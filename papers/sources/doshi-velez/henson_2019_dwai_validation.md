# Digital mental health apps and the therapeutic alliance: initial review

## 📋 Executive Summary
This foundational paper introduces the **Digital Working Alliance Inventory (DWAI)**, the first validated 6-item scale designed to measure the relationship between a patient and a smartphone app. It adapts the traditional Bordin (1979) model (Bond, Task, Goal) for unguided digital interventions.

## 🛠️ Core Methodology
- **Scale Adaptation:** Reduced the 36-item Working Alliance Inventory (WAI) to 6 items (2 per domain).
- **Domain Mapping:**
    - **Goal:** Agreement on what the user wants to achieve.
    - **Task:** Agreement on the activities within the app.
    - **Bond:** Perceived trust and reliability of the technology.

## 📊 Psychometric Properties
- **Sample Size:** Validation across two samples (n = 79, n = 153).
- **Internal Consistency:** Cronbach’s $\alpha = 0.90$.
- **Factor Loading:** A single-factor (unidimensional) structure explains the majority of variance.
- **Correlations:** DWAI scores significantly correlate with app usage frequency and patient satisfaction, but are distinct from simple "Usability."

## 💡 Key Findings
- **The "Bond" exists:** Patients can form a meaningful alliance with software without it being anthropomorphized.
- **Bond vs. Task:** The "Task" domain (usability) is more closely linked to "Bond" in digital settings than in face-to-face therapy.

## 🩺 Clinical Relevance
The DWAI provides a quantitative bridge between digital behavior (telemetry) and the psychological state of the patient. It allows for the optimization of digital interventions based on the quality of the "relationship."
