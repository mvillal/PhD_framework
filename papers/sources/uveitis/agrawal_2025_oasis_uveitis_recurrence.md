# Recurrence Risk Prediction in the OASIS Registry

## 📋 Executive Summary
The study utilizes data from the OASIS registry in Singapore to build predictive models for uveitis recurrence risk, assisting clinicians in long-term patient management.

## 🛠️ Core Methodology
- **Predictive Modeling:** Developed a Random Forest (RF) classifier to estimate the likelihood of disease relapse.
- **Clinical Markers:** Integrated baseline clinical data, including uveitis etiology, anatomical location, and baseline vitreous haze scores.
- **OASIS Registry:** Leveraged a large, longitudinal dataset of uveitis patients from the Singapore National Eye Centre.

## 💡 Key Findings
- **High Specificity:** The RF model achieved a specificity of 0.93 in predicting uveitis recurrence within a 12-month period.
- **Key Predictors:** Identified specific clinical markers and baseline inflammation levels as the most influential factors for predicting relapse.

## 🩺 Clinical Relevance & Impact
- **Clinical Decision Support:** Provides a tool to help clinicians decide on the tapering of medications based on individualized risk scores.
- **Resource Allocation:** Helps prioritize high-risk patients for more frequent monitoring and early intervention.
