# Detection of Posterior Inflammation using UWF Fundus Photography

## 📋 Executive Summary
This research explores the use of Deep Learning on Ultra-Widefield (UWF) fundus photography to detect posterior inflammation in uveitis, acting as a potential surrogate for invasive angiography.

## 🛠️ Core Methodology
- **Deep Learning Framework:** Utilized Google Vertex AI for training and deploying computer vision models.
- **Surrogate for Angiography:** Trained the model to identify clinical features of inflammation usually only visible on Fluorescein Angiography (FA) using standard UWF color fundus images.
- **Dataset:** Stanford Byers Eye Institute uveitis cohort with paired UWF-color and UWF-FA imaging.

## 💡 Key Findings
- **High Sensitivity:** Demonstrated high sensitivity in detecting subclinical posterior inflammation, such as vasculitis and peripheral leakage.
- **Clinical Correlation:** Model predictions showed strong correlation with gold-standard angiographic inflammation scores.

## 🩺 Clinical Relevance & Impact
- **Non-Invasive Monitoring:** Offers a path toward monitoring posterior uveitis activity without the need for repeated dye injections and invasive procedures.
- **Scalable Screening:** Enables automated screening of peripheral inflammation in clinical workflows where FA may not be readily available.
