# Molecular Aging Clock and Proteomic Prediction

## 📋 Executive Summary
Published in *Nature*, this study applies AI to analyze the proteome of the aqueous humor, identifying molecular aging signatures and biomarkers for treatment failure in chronic uveitis.

## 🛠️ Core Methodology
- **High-Throughput Proteomics:** Analyzed the expression levels of over 6,000 proteins in aqueous humor samples using the SomaScan platform.
- **Machine Learning Models:** Used Elastic Net and Random Forest (RF) models to predict patient age and disease state from proteomic profiles.
- **Molecular Aging Clock:** Developed an AI-driven "clock" to measure the biological age of eye tissues relative to chronological age.

## 💡 Key Findings
- **Accelerated Aging:** Chronic uveitis was associated with accelerated molecular aging of the eye's internal environment.
- **Treatment Biomarkers:** Identified specific protein signatures that could predict which patients were likely to fail standard immunosuppressive therapy.

## 🩺 Clinical Relevance & Impact
- **Personalized Medicine:** Informs the development of precision medicine strategies by identifying patients who may require more aggressive initial therapy.
- **Pathophysiological Insight:** Reveals new molecular pathways involved in uveitis progression and ocular aging.
