# AI for Mental Health: Clinician Expectations (Fischer et al., 2025)

## Summary
Reports on a survey of 53 clinicians regarding their expectations and priorities for AI in mental health, revealing a preference for trajectory forecasting and monitoring over explicit treatment prescriptions.

## Methodology
- **Clinician Survey:** A structured survey of 53 mental health professionals (psychiatrists, psychologists, social workers).
- **Preference Elicitation:** Questions designed to identify which types of AI-driven insights clinicians find most valuable.

## Findings
- **Priority on Forecasting:** Clinicians expressed the most interest in AI's ability to forecast patient trajectories (e.g., risk of relapse, symptom progression).
- **Monitoring over Prescription:** There was a clear preference for AI as a "monitoring and alert" tool rather than a system that provides explicit treatment recommendations (prescriptions).

## Relevance
- **Human-Centered AI:** Informs the design of AI systems that align with clinicians' actual needs and clinical workflows.
- **Trust and Adoption:** Suggests that "prescriptive" AI may face more resistance than AI that provides "predictive" or "monitoring" support.

## Key Findings
- **Clinician Priorities:** Identifying where AI can provide the most value according to the end-users themselves.
- **Design Guidelines:** Provides empirical evidence for focusing mental health AI development on trajectory forecasting and risk monitoring.
