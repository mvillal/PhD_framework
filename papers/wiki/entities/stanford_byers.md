# Stanford University / Byers Eye Institute (USA)

## 📋 Overview
The Byers Eye Institute at Stanford University is a major hub for ophthalmic innovation, focusing on ultra-widefield (UWF) imaging, automated inflammation detection, and the integration of proteomics with clinical AI.

## 🔬 Role in Research
- **UWF Inflammation Detection:** Developed AI models for the automated detection and quantification of peripheral inflammation using paired UWF-color and UWF-FA imaging [[Mohammadi et al. (2026)](../../sources/uveitis/mohammadi_2026_uwf_inflammation_detection.md)].
- **Molecular Proteomics:** Leading the way in identifying "molecular aging clocks" and disease biomarkers from ocular fluid analysis [[Mahajan et al. (2023)](../../sources/uveitis/mahajan_2023_molecular_aging_proteomics.md)].

## 📊 High-Signal Metrics & Characteristics
- **Dataset:** Utilizes large cohorts of uveitis patients with comprehensive multi-modal imaging (UWF, OCT, Fundus).
- **Automation:** Transitioning from subjective clinical scales (SUN) to objective metrics via AI-driven image analysis [[Mohammadi et al. (2026)](../../sources/uveitis/mohammadi_2026_uwf_inflammation_detection.md)].
