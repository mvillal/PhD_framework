# Onnela Lab (Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health)

## 🏢 Overview
The Onnela Lab, led by **J.P. Onnela**, is a pioneer in the field of **Digital Phenotyping**. Their research focuses on developing statistical and computational methods to analyze high-dimensional behavioral data from smartphones and other mobile devices.

## 🛠️ Key Platform: Beiwe
The **Beiwe** platform is a specialized, open-source research infrastructure for digital phenotyping. It includes:
- **Passive Sensing:** GPS, accelerometer, gyroscope, Wi-Fi, call and text logs.
- **Active Sensing:** Highly customizable EMA (Ecological Momentary Assessment) surveys and audio recordings.
- **Data Privacy:** On-device encryption and secure cloud storage.

## 🔬 Core Research Focus
- **Statistical Methodology:** N-of-1 causal modeling and non-stationary time series analysis [[Cai & Onnela (2026)](../../sources/onnela/cai_2026_causal_estimands_n_of_1.md)].
- **Clinical Applications:** Monitoring Bipolar Disorder [[Huang et al. (2025)](../../sources/onnela/huang_2025_adolescent_bipolar_monitoring.md)], Schizophrenia, and Suicide Forecasting [[Nock et al. (2026)](../../sources/onnela/nock_2026_smartphone_suicide_prediction.md)].
- **Scalability:** Large-scale epidemiological studies integrating digital footprints with genomics and traditional data.

## 🩺 Clinical Impact
By transforming raw sensor data into "digital biomarkers," the lab provides objective measures of social behavior, mobility, and circadian rhythms, enabling personalized clinical care.
