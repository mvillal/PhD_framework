# UCL Institute of Ophthalmology / Moorfields Eye Hospital

## 🏢 Overview
This partnership between UCL and Moorfields is one of the world's leading centers for ophthalmic research. They are pioneers in the application of **Deep Learning** and **Foundation Models** to retinal imaging, specializing in large-scale dataset integration within the UK National Health Service (NHS).

## 🛠️ Key Technologies: RETFound
- **RETFound (2023):** The first self-supervised foundation model for retinal imaging, pre-trained on 1.6 million images using a Masked Autoencoder (MAE) architecture with 75% masking [[Zhou et al. (2023)](../../../sources/uveitis/zhou_2023_retfound_foundation_model.md)].
- **RETFound-Green (2025):** A smartphone-optimized, "lean" version of the foundation model designed for global health and low-resource settings [[Mortimer et al. (2025)](../../sources/ucl_moorfields/mortimer_2025_retfound_green.md)].
- **Oculomics Platform:** A research infrastructure for detecting systemic diseases (Parkinson's, kidney disease) from retinal scans via "oculomics."

## 🔬 Key Publications & Role in Research
- **Foundation Models:** Pioneered the use of large-scale clinical imaging data for generalizable disease detection, emphasizing rare disease detection with limited labeled data.
- **Global Health:** Focus on generalizability across imaging devices and global patient populations.
- [Zhou et al. (2023): RETFound Foundation Model](../../../sources/uveitis/zhou_2023_retfound_foundation_model.md)
- [Mortimer et al. (2025): RETFound-Green](../../sources/ucl_moorfields/mortimer_2025_retfound_green.md)

## 🩺 Clinical Impact
Their work on foundation models significantly reduces the amount of labeled data required to train ocular AI, enabling rapid deployment of screening tools for rare diseases and in low-resource settings.
