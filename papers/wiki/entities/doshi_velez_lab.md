# Data to Actionable Knowledge Lab (DtAK) - Doshi-Velez Lab

## 📋 Overview
Led by Professor Finale Doshi-Velez at Harvard SEAS, the DtAK lab focuses on making machine learning actionable in real-world, high-stakes settings, with a primary emphasis on healthcare and clinical decision support.

## 🔬 Core Research Pillars
- **Clinical RL Safety:** Developing robust Off-Policy Evaluation (OPE) methods, handled state-action regularization, and addressing the "Deadly Triad" in batch RL [[Gottesman et al. (2019)](../../sources/doshi-velez/gottesman_2019_clinical_rl_guidelines.md)].
- **Human-AI Interaction:** Investigating "Automation Bias," "Human-Labeling Bias," and developing explainable AI (XAI) techniques that provide meaningful insights to clinicians [[Jacobs & Doshi-Velez (2021)](../../sources/doshi-velez/jacobs_2021_antidepressant_bias.md)].
- **Causal Inference in EHR:** Utilizing Structural Causal Models (SCMs) for transportable treatment effect estimation across different clinical sites [[Parbhoo et al. (2022)](../../sources/doshi-velez/parbhoo_2022_causal_ope.md)].
- **Continuous-Time Modeling:** Implementing Neural SDEs and Gaussian Processes for high-frequency risk forecasting in psychiatry [[Lu & Doshi-Velez (2026)](../../sources/doshi-velez/lu_2026_neural_sdes_suicide_risk.md)].

## 📊 Notable Contributions
- **Guidelines for Clinical RL:** Foundational safety manual for researchers [[Gottesman et al. (2019)](../../sources/doshi-velez/gottesman_2019_clinical_rl_guidelines.md)].
- **The Myth of Generalizability:** Identification of site-specific biases in EHR models [[Futoma et al. (2020)](../../sources/doshi-velez/futoma_2020_myth_of_generalisability.md)].
- **Sequential Learning-to-Defer:** A framework for human-AI collaboration [[Joshi et al. (2021)](../../sources/doshi-velez/joshi_2021_learning_to_defer.md)].
