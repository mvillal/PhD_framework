# Circuit Biotypes (Stanford Model)

## 📄 Definition
The **Circuit Biotypes** model, developed by the [[stanford_pmhw]], uses **Brain Imaging (fMRI)** and **Machine Learning** to categorize patients into six distinct biotypes based on the dysfunction of specific neural circuits. This moves psychiatry beyond symptomatic diagnosis (e.g., "Depression") to biological drivers.

## 🛠️ The Six Biotypes
1. **Rumination:** Dysfunction in the default mode circuit; excessive internal focus.
2. **Anxious Apprehension:** Dysfunction in the salience circuit; hyper-vigilance to threat.
3. **Inattention:** Dysfunction in the dorsal attention circuit; inability to focus.
4. **Cognitive Control:** Dysfunction in the frontoparietal circuit; poor executive function.
5. **Negative Affect:** Dysfunction in the amygdala-frontal circuit; persistent sadness.
6. **Anhedonia:** Dysfunction in the reward circuit; inability to feel pleasure.

## 🩺 Clinical Application: Treatment Matching
The biotype model aims to predict treatment response. For example, patients with the "Cognitive Control" biotype may respond better to specific cognitive behavioral therapies, while those with "Reward" biotype dysfunction may require different pharmacological interventions.
