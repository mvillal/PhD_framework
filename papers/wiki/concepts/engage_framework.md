# ENGAGE Framework (2025)

## 📄 Definition
The **ENGAGE Framework** (Effective Next-Generation Architecture for Global Engagement) is a 2025 paradigm shift that moves away from "Usage Maximization" toward "Effective Engagement." It defines the "minimum effective dose" of digital interaction required to foster psychological needs (SDT) and clinical improvement.

## 🛠️ Core Components
- **Effective Engagement Threshold:** The statistically derived level of interaction beyond which clinical returns diminish or negative side effects (digital exhaustion) increase.
- **Intrinsic Motivation Mapping:** Uses [[digital_phenotyping]] to predict a user's current motivational state (Intrinsic, Extrinsic, or Amotivational).
- **Perceived Health Competence (PHC):** The primary psychological outcome variable, measured through both EMA and behavioral progression.

## 🔬 Computational Implementation
- **Motivation Segmenting:** Using LLMs or GMMs to classify users based on their "Interaction Style" (e.g., *Goal-Oriented* vs. *Socially-Driven*).
- **Adaptive Burdens:** Dynamically reducing notification frequency if the model detects "Autonomy Pressure" or low "Autonomy" scores.

## 🩺 Clinical Relevance
The framework is used to validate Digital Therapeutics (DTx) by proving that the digital behavior directly supports the **Digital Working Alliance** between the patient and the intervention.
