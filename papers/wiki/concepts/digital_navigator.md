# Digital Navigator

## 📄 Definition
The **Digital Navigator (DN)** is a standardized human-centered role designed to bridge the gap between psychiatric patients and digital health technologies. First formalized by the [[torous_lab_digital_psychiatry]], the DN acts as a guide, troubleshooting technical issues, personalizing digital workflows, and fostering a **Digital Working Alliance**.

## 🛠️ Role & Evolution
- **Human-Led Triad:** The DN operates as part of a triad: *Patient – Digital Navigator – Clinician*.
- **Agentic AI Hybrid (2024-2026):** Modern DNs use Agentic AI to handle 24/7 monitoring and data parsing, while the human DN focuses on motivational support and "relational accountability."
- **Focus:** Unlike technical support, the DN focuses on **Effective Engagement**—the minimum level of digital interaction required for clinical benefit.

## 🔬 Core Metrics
- **Digital Working Alliance Inventory (DWAI):** Measures the bond, task, and goal alignment between the patient and the app.
- **mARM (Mobile Agnew Relationship Measure):** Assesses the quality of the partnership.
- **Retention Rates:** DN-supported interventions typically show 2x higher retention than unguided apps.

## 🩺 Clinical Application
- **The Digital Clinic:** A model where patients receive "digital prescriptions" and are assigned a DN to ensure successful implementation.
- **Mitigating Digital Exhaustion:** DNs reduce "technostress" by managing notification frequency and personalizing app settings.
