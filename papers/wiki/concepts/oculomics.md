# Oculomics

## 📄 Definition
**Oculomics** is the use of the eye as a non-invasive window into systemic health. By analyzing high-resolution retinal images (fundus, OCT) with machine learning, researchers can identify biomarkers for cardiovascular, renal, and neurodegenerative diseases.

## 🛠️ Key Methodologies
- **Retinal Foundation Models:** Models like **RETFound** (2023) and **RETFound-Green** (2025) that are pre-trained on millions of retinal images to learn a general representation of ocular and systemic health.
- **Feature Discovery:** Using saliency maps or generative models to identify specific retinal vascular or neural patterns associated with systemic conditions like Parkinson's or chronic kidney disease.

## 🩺 Clinical Applications
- **Non-invasive Screening:** Detecting systemic risk during a routine eye exam.
- **Global Health:** Using smartphone-based AI (RETFound-Green) to provide systemic health screening in low-resource settings.
