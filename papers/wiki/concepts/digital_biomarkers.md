# Digital Biomarkers

## 📄 Definition
**Digital Biomarkers** are objective, quantifiable physiological and behavioral data points collected via digital devices (smartphones, wearables) that serve as indicators of health-related outcomes.

## 🛠️ Key Examples in Psychiatry
- **Mobility (GPS):** Location variance, total distance traveled, and time spent at home as indicators of depressive social withdrawal or manic agitation.
- **Communication (Call/Text Logs):** Frequency and duration of interactions as markers of social connectivity.
- **Circadian Rhythms (Accelerometer):** Sleep duration, quality, and onset latency derived from movement data.
- **Cognitive State (Typing Patterns):** Keystroke dynamics and typing speed as indicators of psychomotor agitation or retardation.

## 🔬 Validation Standards
Digital biomarkers must be validated against "gold standard" clinical measures (e.g., PHQ-9 for depression) to ensure they are clinically meaningful.

## 🩺 Clinical Applications
- **Monitoring Disease Progression:** Tracking the slow decline in activity in neurodegenerative diseases.
- **Treatment Efficacy:** Providing objective feedback on whether a treatment is improving a patient's sleep or activity levels.
