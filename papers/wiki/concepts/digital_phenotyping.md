# Digital Phenotyping

## 📄 Definition
**Digital Phenotyping** is the "moment-by-moment quantification of the individual-level human phenotype in situ using data from personal digital devices," particularly smartphones and wearables (Onnela, 2016). It represents a shift from static, episodic clinical assessments to continuous, real-time behavioral monitoring.

## 🛠️ Key Methodologies
- **Passive Sensing:** Unobtrusive data collection from smartphone sensors (GPS, accelerometer, gyroscope, Wi-Fi, call/text logs, screen activity) without requiring user interaction.
- **Active Sensing (EMA):** Ecological Momentary Assessment, where users provide self-reports through surveys or audio recordings at specific times or in response to triggers.
- **Digital Biomarkers:** Objective, quantifiable physiological and behavioral data that serve as indicators of biological processes, pathogenic processes, or responses to an intervention.

## 🔬 Core Platforms
- **Beiwe:** Developed by [[onnela_lab]]. Focuses on high-frequency raw sensor data for statistical research.
- **mindLAMP:** Developed by [[torous_lab_digital_psychiatry]]. Focuses on clinical implementation and patient-facing tools.

## 🩺 Clinical Applications
- **Suicide Forecasting:** Identifying high-risk windows (Hang et al., 2025; Lu et al., 2026; [[Nock et al. (2026)](../../sources/onnela/nock_2026_smartphone_suicide_prediction.md)]).
- **Relapse Prediction:** Detecting manic or depressive episodes in bipolar disorder (Huang et al., 2025).
- **Treatment Optimization:** Using [[n_of_1_modeling]] to monitor patient response to new medications in real-time.

## 🔬 Critical Challenges
- **Data Quality and Missingness:** Addressing "Informative Missingness" (e.g., a patient not using their phone because they are depressed).
- **Privacy and Ethics:** Ensuring patient autonomy and data security under intensive digital surveillance.
- **The Translation Gap:** Turning massive amounts of raw sensor data into actionable clinical insights.
