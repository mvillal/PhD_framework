# Agentic AI in Medicine

## 📄 Definition
**Agentic AI** refers to AI systems capable of autonomous, multi-step reasoning, information gathering, and goal-directed behavior. In medicine, this involves models that don't just "predict," but "act" as diagnostic assistants by conducting patient interviews, ordering tests, and iteratively refining hypotheses.

## 🛠️ Key Methodologies
- **Multi-turn Dialogue:** AI models like **AMIE** (DeepMind, 2024) designed for iterative patient history-taking and clinical reasoning.
- **Uncertainty-Guided Search (UGS):** The ability of a model (e.g., **Med-Gemini**, 2024) to recognize its own knowledge gaps and autonomously query medical literature or patient databases.
- **Autonomous Tool Use:** Models that can trigger external clinical calculators, simulators, or web search tools to ground their reasoning in factual data.

## 🩺 Clinical Applications
- **Automated Triage:** Handling the initial diagnostic interview to prioritize patients for human clinicians.
- **Complex Multi-morbidity Reasoning:** Synthesizing large-scale multimodal data across years of patient history to identify subtle trends.

## 🔬 Critical Challenges
- **Safety and Hallucination:** Ensuring that autonomous reasoning doesn't lead to incorrect or harmful clinical recommendations.
- **Human-in-the-Loop:** Designing systems where the AI agent "defers" to a human clinician at critical decision points.
- **Legal and Ethical Liability:** Determining responsibility when an autonomous agent makes a diagnostic error.
