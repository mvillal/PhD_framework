# NeurIPS 2026 Submission Guidelines

## 📋 Mandatory Paper Checklist
Every submission must include a checklist answering the following (Yes/No/NA + Justification):

### 1. Claims & Limitations
- **Claims:** Do the main claims made in the abstract and introduction accurately reflect the paper's theoretical and empirical results?
- **Limitations:** Have you described the limitations of your work? (e.g., failure cases, assumptions, dataset biases).

### 2. Theory & Proofs
- **Theorems:** Are all theoretical claims (theorems, lemmas, etc.) stated with all necessary assumptions?
- **Proofs:** Are complete proofs provided for all theoretical results? (Usually in the Appendix).

### 3. Experiments & Reproducibility
- **Setup:** Did you include the details of the experimental setup, including hyperparameters?
- **Compute:** Did you report the amount of compute used (e.g., GPU hours) and the type of hardware?
- **Statistical Significance:** Did you report error bars (e.g., standard deviation, confidence intervals)?
- **Data & Code:** Did you include a link to a website, zip file, or similar containing the code and data? (Highly encouraged).

### 4. Ethics & Societal Impact
- **Negative Impacts:** Have you discussed potential negative societal impacts of your work?
- **Ethics Guidelines:** Does your work follow the NeurIPS Code of Ethics?
- **Human Subjects:** If the work involves human subjects, did you describe the consent process and compensation?

## 🖋️ Writing Style & Tone
- **Focus:** Technical novelty, mathematical rigor, and empirical robustness.
- **Notation:** Use standard ML notation (e.g., $\mathcal{D}$ for dataset, $\theta$ for parameters).
- **Structure:** 9 pages max (references and checklist extra).
