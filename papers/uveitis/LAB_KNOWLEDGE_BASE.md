# Uveitis ML Research Knowledge Base

## Core Research Pillars

### 1. Ophthalmic Foundation Models (RETFound)
The development of large-scale foundation models trained on diverse retinal imaging data. These models, such as RETFound, use self-supervised learning (SSL) to learn generalizable features that can be fine-tuned for specific, often rare, ophthalmic conditions like uveitis where labeled data is scarce.

### 2. Objective Grading of Inflammation (OCT/UWF)
Transitioning from subjective clinical scales (e.g., SUN grading) to objective, quantifiable metrics using advanced imaging. This includes automated vitreous haze grading from Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) and the detection of peripheral inflammation using Ultra-Widefield (UWF) fundus photography.

### 3. Molecular Proteomics & Biomarkers
Integrating AI with high-throughput proteomic analysis of ocular fluids (e.g., aqueous humor). This pillar focuses on identifying molecular "aging clocks," treatment failure biomarkers, and systemic disease associations through the analysis of thousands of proteins.

### 4. Infectious Uveitis Detection (Toxoplasmosis, ARN)
Applying deep learning and computer vision to rapidly triage and diagnose infectious uveitis. Using architectures like YOLO and Vision Transformers (ViT) to identify active vs. inactive lesions in conditions such as Ocular Toxoplasmosis and Acute Retinal Necrosis (ARN).

## Key Research Labs

- **UCL / Moorfields Eye Hospital (UK):** Leaders in foundation models (RETFound) and large-scale NHS data integration.
- **Stanford University / Byers Eye Institute (USA):** Innovation in UWF imaging, AI-driven inflammation detection, and molecular proteomics.
- **Singapore National Eye Centre (SNEC):** Large-scale registries (OASIS) and recurrence risk prediction models.
- **Wenzhou Medical University (China):** Advancements in automated grading and multi-modal imaging analysis.
