# Ocular Toxoplasmosis Four-class Diagnosis (Umer et al., 2025)

## Summary
This paper details the development of a diagnostic system for Ocular Toxoplasmosis using YOLOv11 and Vision Transformers (ViT) to distinguish between different stages of lesion activity.

## Methodology
- **YOLOv11 Object Detection:** Used for the rapid detection and localization of toxoplasmosis lesions in fundus images.
- **Vision Transformers (ViT):** Employed ViT architectures for the fine-grained classification of lesions into four classes: Active, Inactive, Scarred, and Non-toxo.
- **Ensemble Approach:** Combined the speed of YOLO with the high-resolution feature extraction of ViT for robust performance.

## Findings
- **High Diagnostic Accuracy:** The system demonstrated high accuracy in identifying active lesions, which require immediate treatment.
- **Automated Triage:** Provided a tool capable of rapidly triaging infectious uveitis cases in resource-limited settings.

## Relevance
- **Infectious Uveitis Management:** Crucial for the timely diagnosis of ocular toxoplasmosis, which can lead to rapid vision loss if left untreated.
- **Scalable Diagnostics:** Enables automated screening of infectious cases, reducing the workload on specialized uveitis clinics.
