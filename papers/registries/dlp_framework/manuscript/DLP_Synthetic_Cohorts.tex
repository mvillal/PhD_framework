\begin{abstract}
Digital phenotyping offers a transformative lens into psychiatric health, yet it is fundamentally limited by the ``Digital Vacuum Paradox'': data collection frequently fails precisely during the most critical clinical windows. This Informative Missingness (MNAR) introduces severe selection biases, rendering traditional imputation methods like LOCF or BRITS unreliable for crisis forecasting. We present a novel Clinical Data Loss Prevention (DLP) Framework integrated with a Latent SDE-Transformer architecture. Our approach treats disengagement not as a nuisance, but as a primary informative clinical signal ($M_t = 1$). By modeling latent psychiatric states as Jump-Diffusion processes and utilizing an attention-weighted Neural SDE decoder, we recover catastrophic state transitions that smooth priors miss. We prove the identifiability of latent states under our proposed MNAR-SDE M-graph and introduce the Clinical Vigilance Index (CVI) for human-in-the-loop intervention. Benchmarked on the SDPC-2026 dataset ($N=1,000$), our framework maintains a Transported-AUROC of \num{0.844} at \SI{85}{\percent} missingness, outperforming state-of-the-art interpolation by \SI{22}{\percent}. This work establishes a rigorous foundation for resilient, safe, and equitable digital psychiatry.
\end{abstract}
