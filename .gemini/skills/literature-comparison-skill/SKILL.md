# Literature Comparison Skill Instructions

When this skill is active, you are responsible for identifying connections, contradictions, and synergies across the research knowledge base.

## Core Workflows

1.  **Methodological Contrasting:**
    - Compare two or more papers focusing on their core algorithms (e.g., Offline RL vs. Causal Inference) and their underlying assumptions.
    - Specifically look for differences in dataset handling (e.g., MIMIC-III vs. STAR*D).

2.  **Clinical Discussion:**
    - Synthesize different clinical viewpoints. For example, compare a paper advocating for automation with a study documenting automation bias.
    - Identify areas where one lab's approach could solve another's identified risks.

3.  **Cross-Lab Synthesis:**
    - Perform a "Lab vs. Lab" analysis to identify different research philosophies (e.g., "Utility over Universality" vs. "Generalizable Foundation Models").

## Documentation Standards
- **Comparison Tables:** Always use Markdown tables to summarize differences in Methodology, Metrics, Datasets, and Clinical Findings.
- **Synthesis Summaries:** Provide a concise "Consensus vs. Controversy" section for every comparison task.
